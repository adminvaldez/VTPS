Power Washing vs Pressure Washing

While often used interchangeably, these two methods serve different purposes depending on surface type, contamination level, and material durability.

PW

Power Washing

Power washing uses **heated water combined with pressure** to remove grease, oil, mold, algae, and heavy organic buildup.

Best Used For:
  • Oil-stained concrete
  • Commercial parking lots
  • Industrial surfaces
  • Heavy mildew buildup
Industry Recommendation:

Industry standards recommend heated water for removing oil and biological growth, as heat improves cleaning efficiency while reducing chemical dependency.

PR

Pressure Washing

Pressure washing uses **high-pressure water without heat** to clean surfaces effectively while minimizing risk to delicate materials.

Best Used For:
  • Driveways & sidewalks
  • Brick & stone surfaces
  • Patios
  • Fences
Industry Recommendation:

For residential properties, pressure washing with controlled PSI settings is recommended to prevent surface etching and material damage.

Soft Washing (Low-Pressure Method)

The preferred industry standard for siding, roofs, and painted surfaces.

Soft washing uses low pressure combined with biodegradable cleaning solutions to safely eliminate mold, algae, and bacteria at the root. This method prevents damage while extending surface lifespan.

Recommended For:
  • Vinyl siding
  • Stucco
  • Roof shingles
  • Painted wood
